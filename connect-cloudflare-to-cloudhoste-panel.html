Connect Cloudflare to CloudHoste Panel Website

Cloud computing has been taking online services by storm. Regardless of whether you have a small-to-medium business or manage an established enterprise with hundreds of servers – cloud solutions should already be a part of your strategy.

As many as 98% of online organizations utilize the technology in some form, most even employing a multi-cloud environment in their everyday operations. Google, Facebook, Apple—there is no tech giant that doesn’t heavily rely on the cloud.

This is far from surprising – the immense scalability, redundancy, and data security are unmatched by any other existing tech. When you take into account the associated cost and resource savings – the decision to adopt the cloud becomes a no-brainer.

What is Cloudflare?

Cloudflare is the leading content delivery network (CDN) company in the world. The provider manages over 12,500 servers across six continents, connecting people worldwide with the cloud.

Cloudflare was founded in San Francisco in 2009 by three entrepreneurs – Lee Holloway, Matthew Prince, and Michelle Zatlyn. What followed were years of growth and acquisitions, including web and security companies like StopTheHacker, CryptoSeal, and Neumob.

Today, the CDN giant boasts over $1.2 billion in revenues and an operating income of around $185 million. Even though the company offers a free tier of services (used by millions), it still has over 210,000 paying customers, with a few thousand of the biggest online businesses trusting its cloud network. A little over 10% of Cloudflare’s customer base is from the United States, followed by the United Kingdom, Brazil, and Germany.

Currently, the company employs close to 4,000 people.

How Can Cloudflare Help Your Website?

Every content delivery system aims to provide access to a global network of servers you can utilize for more flexibility and reach. Cloudflare is no different. Your website can benefit in numerous ways by employing its services:

Network Reach

Employing servers all over the world means visitors get the same high performance thanks to nearby servers, regardless of their location.

Budgeted Solution

Cloudflare offers a Free Tier plan packed with essentials to start with. You can easily upgrade as your business expands.

Next-level Security

Mitigate DDoS attacks easily with a Web Application Firewall (WAF) and connections secured via SSL/TLS encryption.

Better Performance

Cached copies of your content on global edge servers guarantee faster load times and happier customers.

Redundant Environment

No single point of failure. If one edge server goes down, another takes over instantly for uninterrupted uptime.

Image Optimization

Automatically resizes images and presents them in optimal modern formats for faster load times.

How to Integrate Cloudflare in CloudHoste Panel: Step-by-Step Guide

CloudHoste Panel is the unique web hosting management system developed by our team at CloudHoste. It serves as a one-stop solution for customers to operate their websites, domains, emails, and all other aspects of their online hosting journey.

Integrating and managing Cloudflare through CloudHoste Panel is designed to be as simplified as possible. Follow this step-by-step guide to get it up and running:

Step 1
Access DNS Editor

Log into your CloudHoste Panel dashboard. Under the Tools section, locate and select the DNS Editor option.

Step 2
Select Domain

Once inside the DNS Editor, select the Domain you wish to connect to the CDN and click the blue Enable Cloudflare button at the top right of the page.

Step 3
View Cloudflare API Guidelines

A popup modal will appear on your screen showing detailed instructions on the specific API Token permissions required from your Cloudflare account to run the integration.

Step 4
Generate Token on Cloudflare

Open a new browser tab and sign into your Cloudflare Dashboard. Then follow these instructions:

  • Click your User Icon on the top right corner and click My Profile.
  • On the left-hand sidebar, select API Tokens, then click Create Token.
  • Choose the Use Template option next to the Edit Zone DNS template.
Step 5
Configure Token Permissions

On the configuration page, you must define the following token settings for your DNS zone.

Important Note: By default, you will only see one permission line. Click on the + Add More button to append the rest.
Permission Path Access Level
Zone / Zone Edit
Zone / DNS Edit
Zone / Zone Settings Edit
Zone / Cache Purge Purge
User / User Details Read

Under the Zone Resources section, change the resource drop-down from Specific Zone to All Zones, then save and proceed to view the token summary.

Step 6
Paste API Token in CloudHoste Panel

Once Cloudflare configures the token, copy the generated API Token. Switch back to your CloudHoste Panel dashboard tab and paste the token code at the bottom of the popup form. Click verify to link the integrations together.

Integrated Dashboard Options

Once activated, the API token reveals advanced DNS control panel features directly inside your CloudHoste Panel:

Sync With Cloudflare

Toggle Cloudflare CDN off or on for individual domains to easily fall back on standard server DNS records when required.

"I'm Under Attack" Mode

Instantly activate aggressive security filtering to combat heavy DDoS attacks and protect your server resources from overload.

Development Mode

Bypasses standard CDN caches immediately, enabling you to inspect file updates on your site in real-time.

Purge CDN Cache

Clears edge cache storage instantly. Ideal for forcing static resource updates to propagate globally.

Furthermore, you can toggle the Proxy Status button to mask the server IP address behind your domain, preventing direct-to-IP targeted attacks. You can also click Edit to modify server records (A, AAAA, CNAME, etc.) instantly, synchronizing changes in real-time.

As you can see, CloudHoste Panel makes Cloudflare management quick and easy without any previous technical experience. If you need any additional assistance – do not hesitate to contact our Support Team 24/7 through live chat, email, or ticketing.
