Getting Started with CloudHoste WordPress Manager

WordPress is by far and away the most popular Content Management System (CMS) in the world. What started off as a simple blog platform now powers more than a third of the world’s websites, and it’s not difficult to see why.

Although it wasn’t the first CMS in the world, WordPress proved to be one of the most versatile and easy-to-use platforms of its kind, and, partly thanks to the fact that it’s free, people quickly adopted it. Despite the appearance of numerous point-and-click website builders, its popularity hasn’t dwindled, and it’s still considered the go-to solution for anyone trying to launch a new project.

What is CloudHoste WordPress Manager?

Inexperienced website administrators overlook some important factors when working with WordPress, and even seasoned developers don’t always bother with vital aspects of the platform’s configuration. As a result, many online businesses suffer from performance issues and cyberattacks. Manually installing and properly configuring WordPress can indeed be tricky. Fortunately, CloudHoste customers can take advantage of our CloudHoste WordPress Manager.

CloudHoste WordPress Manager is a WordPress management tool that is integrated into CloudHoste Panel and is available free of charge to anyone who picks one of CloudHoste’s managed cloud VPS hosting solutions. It can install WordPress on your account, and it can also help you configure and secure it properly.

Managing content with WordPress might be an easy task, but getting the platform going in the first place normally requires a certain level of expertise. With the WordPress Manager, however, you simply need to pick the location and login credentials, and your installation will be ready in seconds.

Installing WordPress with CloudHoste WordPress Manager

To install a WordPress site using the manager, log into your account's CloudHoste Panel control panel and navigate to WordPress Manager. At the bottom of the page, you’ll see a list of all existing WordPress installations, and above it is the form for creating a new one:

Step 1
Choose Location

From the drop-down menu, pick your main domain name, one of your addon domains, or a subdomain. If you want to install in a subfolder (e.g., domain.com/blog), specify this in the Folder name field.

Step 2
Administrator Credentials

Pick the username and password for the WordPress administrator. The password must be at least eight characters long and contain at least one letter, one digit, and one symbol.

Step 3
Set Admin Email

Fill in the administrator’s email address. To help you pick a unique password, there is an integrated password generator you can use directly from the field.

Step 4
Install WordPress

Click the Install WordPress button. In a few seconds, the process will complete, and the new installation will appear in the list at the bottom of the page.

Configuring automatic updates and Security Lock

Before logging in, you might want to look at a couple of toggle switches located next to your new WordPress installation:

Auto Updates

Enabled by default. Keeping it enabled ensures that WordPress core, themes, and plugins will automatically apply updates. This secures security vulnerabilities and keeps performance at its peak without human intervention.

Security Lock

Limits write access to your directories. Even if hackers find a theme or plugin exploit, they won't be able to insert malicious files. WordPress Manager handles updates by automatically unlocking this feature temporarily and re-locking it once done.

Cloning, deleting, and changing passwords of WordPress installations

Next to the Auto Updates and Security Lock toggles is the Actions drop-down menu which gives you more advanced utilities:

1
Change Admin Passwords

Change your admin password directly from the Control Panel without knowing the old password. Excellent for regaining access or locking out unauthorized users.

2
Clone Installations

Replicate your exact WordPress site (files, layouts, settings, and database) into any subdirectory or domain. Very useful when testing new designs in subdomains before cloning them back to the live domain.

3
Uninstall Websites

Easily remove WordPress installations and delete their corresponding databases from the server with just a couple of clicks by choosing Uninstall.

Conclusion

WordPress may be pretty intuitive to work with, but certain aspects of its installation and configuration might present a challenge without additional help. With the tools provided by CloudHoste WordPress Manager, you will be able to focus on your business while knowing that your website is properly configured and secured.
